Circle Inscribed Angle Theorem

Circle Geometry
  1. For a given arc, the inscribed angle subtended by that arc is constant and equals half the central angle subtended by the same arc.
Inscribed angle is half the central angle for the same arc.
ⓘ If the central angle for a semicircle is \(180^\circ\), the inscribed angle is \(90^\circ\).
\( \text{Inscribed angle} = \dfrac{1}{2} \times \text{Central angle} \)
