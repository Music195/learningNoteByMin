Converse of the Inscribed Angle Theorem

Circle Geometry
Given four points A, B, P, Q such that points P and Q are on the same side of line AB,
if \(\angle APB = \angle AQB\),
then the four points A, B, P, Q lie on the same circle (are concyclic).
If \(\angle APB = \angle AQB\), then A, B, P, Q are concyclic.
