Riemann Sum and Definite Integral
\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{k=1}^n f(x_k)\Delta x = \int_a^b \! \color{#ff7043}{f(x)}\,dx \)

Here,
\( \displaystyle \Delta x = \frac{b-a}{n} \),   \( \displaystyle x_k = a + k\Delta x \)
In the above formula, especially when \( a=0,\, b=1 \),
the following equation holds.
\( \displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{k=1}^n f\left(\frac{k}{n}\right) = \int_0^1 f(x)\,dx \)
