Area Bounded by the Curve \( y=f(x) \)

Application of Integration
The area \( S \) bounded by the two lines \( x=a \), \( x=b \) is:
\( S = \int_a^b \color{#ff7043}{f(x)}\,dx \)
In the above formula,
if \( f(x) \leq 0 \) for all \( x \) in the interval \( [a, b] \),
\( S \) is given by the following formula:
\( S = \int_a^b \color{#2196f3}{\{-f(x)\}}\,dx \)
