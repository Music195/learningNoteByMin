Distance Traveled on the Coordinate Plane

Application of Integration
Let a point \( P(x, y) \) move on the coordinate plane, where the coordinates \( x \) and \( y \) are functions of time \( t \).
The distance \( s \) that \( P \) travels from time \( t_1 \) to \( t_2 \) is:
\( s = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} \sqrt{\left(\frac{dx}{dt}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{dy}{dt}\right)^2} \, dt = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} |\vec{v}| dt \)
