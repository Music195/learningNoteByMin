3. Properties of Limits of Sequences 2
Suppose the sequences $\{a_n\}$ and $\{b_n\}$ both converge, with
$\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \alpha$,   $\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = \beta$.
5 For all $n$, if $a_n \leq b_n$ then $\alpha \leq \beta$
6 For all $n$, if $a_n \leq c_n \leq b_n$ and $\alpha = \beta$, then $\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} c_n = \alpha$
  • In property 5, it is possible that $a_n < b_n$ for all $n$, but $\alpha = \beta$.
  • Property 6 is known as the Squeeze Theorem (Sandwich Theorem).
