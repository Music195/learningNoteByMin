Quotient Rule for Derivatives
If both $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ are differentiable, then:
  1. $\displaystyle \left\{ \frac{1}{g(x)} \right\}' = $ $-\frac{g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2}$
  2. $\displaystyle \left\{ \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right\}' = $ $\frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2}$
The quotient rule allows you to differentiate the quotient of two differentiable functions.
