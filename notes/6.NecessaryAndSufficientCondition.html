Necessary and Sufficient Conditions
If the statement $p \Rightarrow q$ is true, then $p$ is a sufficient condition for $q$, and $q$ is a necessary condition for $p$.
If both $p \Rightarrow q$ and $q \Rightarrow p$ are true, then $p$ is a necessary and sufficient condition for $q$.
💡
When $p \Rightarrow q$ is true:
To satisfy $q$, it is sufficient to satisfy $p$.
To satisfy $p$, it is necessary to satisfy $q$.
