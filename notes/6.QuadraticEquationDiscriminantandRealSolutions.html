Discriminant and Number of Real Solutions
For the quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, the discriminant is defined as:
$D = b^2 - 4ac$
  • $D > 0$   ↔   There are two distinct real solutions.
  • $D = 0$   ↔   There is exactly one real solution (a repeated root).
  • $D < 0$   ↔   There are no real solutions.
In particular, $D \geq 0$ ↔ "There is at least one real solution."
