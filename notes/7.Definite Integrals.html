Definite Integrals

Integration
Let \( f(x) \) be a continuous function on an interval, and let \( F(x) \) be an antiderivative of \( f(x) \). For any \( a, b \) in the interval:
\( \displaystyle \int_a^b f(x)\, dx = \left[ F(x) \right]_a^b = \) \( F(b) - F(a) \)
Note: If \( f(x) \geq 0 \) on \([a, b]\), then \( \int_a^b f(x)\, dx \) represents the area under the curve \( y = f(x) \) between \( x = a \) and \( x = b \).
