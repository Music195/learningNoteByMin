Menelaus' Theorem (メネラウスの定理)

Triangle Geometry
In triangle ABC, let a line ℓ (not passing through any vertex) intersect the sides BC, CA, AB (or their extensions) at points P, Q, R as shown.

Menelaus' Theorem states:
\( \displaystyle \frac{BP}{PC} \cdot \frac{CQ}{QA} \cdot \frac{AR}{RB} = 1 \)
The line ℓ intersects the sides (or their extensions) at P, Q, R.
