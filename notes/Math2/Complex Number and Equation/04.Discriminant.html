Discriminant and Types of Solutions
For the quadratic equation \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \), let \( D = b^2 - 4ac \):

\( D > 0 \iff \) Two distinct real solutions
\( D = 0 \iff \) One repeated real solution
\( D < 0 \iff \) Two distinct complex solutions
The discriminant \( D \) determines the nature of the solutions of a quadratic equation. If \( D \geq 0 \), the solutions are real; otherwise, they are complex.
