Quadratic Factorization
For the quadratic equation \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \), let the roots be \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \):

\( ax^2 + bx + c = a(x - \alpha)(x - \beta) \)
A quadratic equation with real coefficients can always be factored into the product of two linear factors in the field of complex numbers.
