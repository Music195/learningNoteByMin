Relationship Between Roots and Coefficients (Cubic)
For the cubic equation \( ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d = 0 \), let the roots be \( \alpha, \beta, \gamma \):

\( \alpha + \beta + \gamma = \frac{-b}{a} \)

\( \alpha\beta + \beta\gamma + \gamma\alpha = \frac{c}{a} \)

\( \alpha\beta\gamma = \frac{-d}{a} \)
The relationships between the roots and coefficients of a cubic equation extend the quadratic case, involving sums, products, and pairwise products of the roots.
