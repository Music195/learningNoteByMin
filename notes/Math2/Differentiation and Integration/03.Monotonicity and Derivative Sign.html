Monotonicity and the Sign of \( f'(x) \)
On an interval, the behavior of \( f(x) \) is determined by the sign of its derivative \( f'(x) \):
If \( f'(x) > 0 \) always, then \( f(x) \) is increasing on that interval.
If \( f'(x) < 0 \) always, then \( f(x) \) is decreasing on that interval.
If \( f'(x) = 0 \) always, then \( f(x) \) is constant on that interval.
