Exponent Laws
For \( a \neq 0, b \neq 0 \), and integers \( m, n \):
1. \( a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n} \)
2. \( \dfrac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n} \)
3. \( (a^m)^n = a^{mn} \)
4. \( (ab)^n = a^n b^n \)
These laws also apply when the exponent is a rational number.
Example: \( 3^5, \; 3^{-2} \)
\( 3^5 \times 3^{-2} = 3^{5+(-2)} = 3^3 \)
\( \dfrac{3^5}{3^2} = 3^{5-2} = 3^3 \)
\( (3^5)^{-2} = 3^{5 \times (-2)} = 3^{-10} = \dfrac{1}{3^{10}} \)
\( (3 \times 5)^{-2} = \dfrac{1}{(3 \times 5)^2} = \dfrac{1}{3^2 \times 5^2} = 3^{-2} \times 5^{-2} \)
