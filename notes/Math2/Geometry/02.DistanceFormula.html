Distance Formula
The distance between two points \( A(x_1, y_1) \) and \( B(x_2, y_2) \) is:

\( AB = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \)

Specifically, the distance from the origin \( O \) to a point \( A(x_1, y_1) \) is:

\( OA = \sqrt{x_1^2 + y_1^2} \)
The distance formula is derived using the Pythagorean theorem. It holds true even when the line segment is parallel to the x-axis or y-axis.
