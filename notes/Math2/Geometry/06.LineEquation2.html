Equation of a Line (Two-Point Form)
For two distinct points \( (x_1, y_1) \) and \( (x_2, y_2) \):

If \( x_1 \neq x_2 \), the equation of the line passing through these points is:
\( y - y_1 = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}(x - x_1) \)

If \( x_1 = x_2 \), the equation of the line is:
\( x = x_1 \)
The two-point form is useful for finding the equation of a line when two points on the line are known.
