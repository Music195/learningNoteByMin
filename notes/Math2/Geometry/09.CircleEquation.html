Equation of a Circle
The equation of a circle with center \( (a, b) \) and radius \( r \) is:

\( (x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 = r^2 \)

Specifically, for a circle centered at the origin with radius \( r \):

\( x^2 + y^2 = r^2 \)
The equation of a circle is derived from the Pythagorean theorem, relating the radius and coordinates.
