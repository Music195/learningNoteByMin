Position Relationship Between a Circle and a Line
The position relationship between a circle and a line can be determined by solving the quadratic equation \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \) obtained by eliminating \( y \) from the equations of the circle and the line.

The discriminant \( D = b^2 - 4ac \) determines the relationship:
Discriminant \( D \) Relationship Diagram
\( D > 0 \) The line intersects the circle at two distinct points.
\( D = 0 \) The line is tangent to the circle (touches at one point).
\( D < 0 \) The line does not intersect the circle.
The discriminant \( D \) helps classify the relationship between the circle and the line based on the number of real solutions.
