Linear Inequalities and Regions
1. The region represented by the inequality \( y > mx + n \) is the **upper side** of the line \( y = mx + n \).

2. The region represented by the inequality \( y < mx + n \) is the **lower side** of the line \( y = mx + n \).

Additionally, the regions represented by \( y \geq mx + n \) or \( y \leq mx + n \) include the line \( y = mx + n \) itself.
The boundary line \( y = mx + n \) divides the plane into two regions, and the inequality determines which side is included.
