Circle and Regions
1. The region represented by the inequality \( x^2 + y^2 < r^2 \) is the **interior** of the circle \( x^2 + y^2 = r^2 \).

2. The region represented by the inequality \( x^2 + y^2 > r^2 \) is the **exterior** of the circle \( x^2 + y^2 = r^2 \).

Additionally, the regions represented by \( x^2 + y^2 \leq r^2 \) or \( x^2 + y^2 \geq r^2 \) include the circle \( x^2 + y^2 = r^2 \) itself.
The circle \( x^2 + y^2 = r^2 \) divides the plane into two regions: the interior and the exterior. The inequality determines which region is included.
