Radians and Degrees
In a circle with a radius of 1, the central angle corresponding to an arc length of 1 is defined as **1 radian**.

- \( 180^\circ = \pi \) radians
- \( 1 \) radian \( = \frac{180}{\pi}^\circ \)

The method of expressing angles in degrees is called the **degree system**, while the method of expressing angles in radians is called the **radian system**.
Radians provide a natural way to measure angles based on the arc length of a circle. This is especially useful in trigonometry and calculus.
