Graphs of Trigonometric Functions
The graphs of the sine, cosine, and tangent functions are shown below. These functions have distinct properties such as periodicity, range, and symmetry.
Function Period Range Symmetry
\( y = \sin \theta \) \( 2\pi \) \( -1 \leq y \leq 1 \) Symmetric about the origin
\( y = \cos \theta \) \( 2\pi \) \( -1 \leq y \leq 1 \) Symmetric about the \( y \)-axis
\( y = \tan \theta \) \( \pi \) All real numbers Symmetric about the origin
The sine and cosine functions are periodic with a period of \( 2\pi \), while the tangent function has a period of \( \pi \). These properties are fundamental in trigonometry.
