Velocity and Acceleration (Plane)

Application of Differentiation
When the position of a point \( P(x, y) \) moving in the plane at time \( t \) is given by \( x = x(t) \), \( y = y(t) \),
the velocity vector \( \vec{v} \), its magnitude \( |\vec{v}| \), the acceleration vector \( \vec{a} \), and its magnitude \( |\vec{a}| \) at time \( t \) are:
\( \vec{v} = \left( \dfrac{dx}{dt},\ \dfrac{dy}{dt} \right) \quad |\vec{v}| = \sqrt{ \left( \dfrac{dx}{dt} \right)^2 + \left( \dfrac{dy}{dt} \right)^2 } \)
\( \vec{a} = \left( \dfrac{d^2x}{dt^2},\ \dfrac{d^2y}{dt^2} \right) \quad |\vec{a}| = \sqrt{ \left( \dfrac{d^2x}{dt^2} \right)^2 + \left( \dfrac{d^2y}{dt^2} \right)^2 } \)
Note: The velocity vector gives the direction and speed of motion, and the acceleration vector gives the rate of change of velocity.
