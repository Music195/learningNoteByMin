Mean Value Theorem

Application of Differentiation
If a function \( f(x) \) is continuous on the closed interval \( [a, b] \) and differentiable on the open interval \( (a, b) \), then there exists at least one real number \( c \) with \( a < c < b \) such that:
\( \displaystyle \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} = f'(c), \quad a < c < b \)
This theorem means: For any two points A and B on the curve \( y = f(x) \), there is at least one point C between A and B where the tangent to the curve is parallel to the secant line AB.
In other words, the instantaneous rate of change at some point equals the average rate of change over the interval.
