Necessary Condition for Local Extrema

Application of Differentiation
If \( f(x) \) is differentiable at \( x = a \) and \( f(x) \) has a local maximum or minimum at \( x = a \), then
\( f'(a) = 0 \)
Note: The converse does not necessarily hold.
That is, even if \( f'(a) = 0 \), it does not always mean that \( f(x) \) has a local extremum at \( x = a \).
