Sign of the Second Derivative and Concavity

Application of Differentiation
Suppose a function \( f(x) \) has a second derivative \( f''(x) \). Then:
  1. If \( f''(x) > 0 \) on an interval, the curve \( y = f(x) \) is concave upward (convex down) on that interval.
  2. If \( f''(x) < 0 \) on an interval, the curve \( y = f(x) \) is concave downward (convex up) on that interval.
Explanation:
1. If \( f''(x) > 0 \), then \( f'(x) \) is increasing, so the slope of the tangent line increases and the curve is concave upward.
2. If \( f''(x) < 0 \), then \( f'(x) \) is decreasing, so the slope of the tangent line decreases and the curve is concave downward.
