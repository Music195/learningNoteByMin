Second Derivative Test for Extrema

Application of Differentiation
Suppose \( f''(x) \) is continuous.
  1. If \( f'(a) = 0 \) and \( f''(a) > 0 \), then \( f(a) \) is a local minimum.
  2. If \( f'(a) = 0 \) and \( f''(a) < 0 \), then \( f(a) \) is a local maximum.
Note: If \( f'(a) = 0 \) and \( f''(a) = 0 \), then \( f(a) \) may or may not be an extremum.
