Derivatives of Logarithmic and Exponential Functions
Logarithmic Functions

Let $a$ be a positive constant not equal to $1$:

$(\log|x|)' = $ $\dfrac{1}{x}$   $(\log_a|x|)' = $ $\dfrac{1}{x \log a}$

In general:
$\displaystyle \left\{ \log |f(x)| \right\}' = $ $\dfrac{f'(x)}{f(x)}$
The derivative of the logarithm of a function is the derivative of the inside divided by the inside itself.
Exponential Functions

$(e^x)' = $ $e^x$   $(a^x)' = $ $a^x \log a$
The derivative of $e^x$ is itself. The derivative of $a^x$ (for $a > 0$) is $a^x$ times $\log a$.
