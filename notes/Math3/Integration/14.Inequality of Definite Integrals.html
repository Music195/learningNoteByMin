Inequality of Definite Integrals

Application of Integration
For continuous functions $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ on the interval $[a, b]$:
If $f(x) \geq g(x)$ for all $x$ in $[a, b]$, then
\[ \int_a^b \color{#ff7043}{f(x)}\,dx \geq \int_a^b \color{#ff7043}{g(x)}\,dx \]
Equality holds if and only if $f(x) = g(x)$ everywhere on $[a, b]$.
Explanation:
If one function is always greater than or equal to another on a closed interval, then the area under its curve (the definite integral) is also greater than or equal to the area under the other.

This is a fundamental property of definite integrals and is widely used in calculus and analysis.
