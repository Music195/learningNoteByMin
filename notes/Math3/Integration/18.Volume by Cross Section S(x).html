Volume by Cross Section \( S(x) \)

Application of Integration
Let the intersection points of two planes perpendicular to the x-axis, \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \), be \( a \) and \( b \), respectively.
If the cross-sectional area of the solid cut by a plane \( \gamma \) perpendicular to the x-axis at \( x \) is \( S(x) \), then the volume \( V \) between the two planes is:
\( V = \int_a^b \color{#ff7043}{S(x)}\,dx \)
where \( a < b \).
