Volume of Solid of Revolution about x-axis

Application of Integration
The region bounded by the curve \( y = f(x) \), the x-axis, and the lines \( x = a \), \( x = b \) is rotated once about the x-axis.
The volume \( V \) of the resulting solid of revolution is:
\( V = \pi \int_a^b \color{#ff7043}{\{f(x)\}^2} dx = \pi \int_a^b y^2 dx \)
where \( a < b \).
