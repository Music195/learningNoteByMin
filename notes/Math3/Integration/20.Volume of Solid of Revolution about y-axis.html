Volume of Solid of Revolution about y-axis

Application of Integration
The region bounded by the curve \( x = g(y) \), the y-axis, and the lines \( y = c \), \( y = d \) is rotated once about the y-axis.
The volume \( V \) of the resulting solid of revolution is:
\( V = \pi \int_c^d \color{#ff7043}{\{g(y)\}^2} dy = \pi \int_c^d x^2 dy \)
where \( c < d \).
