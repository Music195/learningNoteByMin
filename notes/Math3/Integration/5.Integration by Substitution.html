Integration by Substitution

Integration
1. \( \displaystyle \int f(x)\, dx = \int f(g(t))\, g'(t)\, dt \)   where \( x = g(t) \)
2. \( \displaystyle \int f(g(x))\, g'(x)\, dx = \int f(u)\, du \)   where \( u = g(x) \)
3. \( \displaystyle \int \frac{g'(x)}{g(x)}\, dx = \) \( \log|g(x)| + C \)
Tip: When \( x = g(t) \), then \( \frac{dx}{dt} = g'(t) \), so \( dx = g'(t)\,dt \).
This allows you to rewrite \( \int f(x)\, dx \) as \( \int f(g(t))\, g'(t)\, dt \), which can make integration easier.
Similarly, for \( u = g(x) \), \( du = g'(x)\, dx \).
