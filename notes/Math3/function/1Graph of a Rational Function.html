1. Graph of a Rational Function
1  The graph of the rational function $y = \dfrac{k}{x-p} + q$ is
$y = \dfrac{k}{x}$ shifted $p$ units$ in the $x$-direction and $q$ units$ in the $y$-direction.
The asymptotes are $x = p$ and $y = q$
2  The domain is $x \neq p$, and the range is $y \neq q$
To sketch the graph of $y = \dfrac{k}{x-p} + q$, treat the point $(p, q)$ as the new origin and draw the graph of $y = \dfrac{k}{x}$.
