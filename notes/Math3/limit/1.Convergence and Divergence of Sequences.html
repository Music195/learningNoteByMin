1. Convergence and Divergence of Sequences
Convergence
  • Converges to a value $\alpha$
Divergence
  • Diverges to $+\infty$ (positive infinity)
  • Diverges to $-\infty$ (negative infinity)
  • Oscillates (no limit)
$\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \alpha$ ($\alpha$)
$\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \infty$ ($+\infty$)
$\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = -\infty$ ($-\infty$)
No limit (none)
Note: If a sequence does not converge to a finite value, it is said to diverge (either to infinity, negative infinity, or oscillates).
