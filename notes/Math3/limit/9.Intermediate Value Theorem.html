Intermediate Value Theorem

Suppose a function \( f(x) \) is continuous on the closed interval \( [a, b] \), and \( f(a) \neq f(b) \).
For any value \( k \) between \( f(a) \) and \( f(b) \), there exists at least one real number \( c \) with \( a < c < b \) such that
\( f(c) = \) \( k \) ,   \( a < c < b \)
Note:
As a consequence, if \( f(x) \) is continuous on \( [a, b] \) and \( f(a) \) and \( f(b) \) have opposite signs, then the equation \( f(x) = 0 \) has at least one real solution in \( (a, b) \).
