Congruence Rules

Properties of Congruence Modulo m
If a ≡ c (mod m) and b ≡ d (mod m), then:
  1. 1 a + b ≡ c + d (mod m)
  2. 2 a − b ≡ c − d (mod m)
  3. 3 ab ≡ cd (mod m)
  4. 4 ak ≡ ck (mod m)
For two integers a and c, we say they are congruent modulo m (i.e., a - c is a multiple of m) and write a ≡ c (mod m).
