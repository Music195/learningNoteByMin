Counting Permutations

Number of Ways to Arrange r Objects from n
The number of ways to arrange r objects from n distinct objects (permutations) is:
nPr = n (n − 1) (n − 2) ⋯⋯ (n − r + 1)
Example: How many ways to arrange 3 out of 4 objects?
Ways to choose the 1st object: 4 Ways to choose the 2nd object: 4 − 1 Ways to choose the 3rd object: 4 − 2 So, by the multiplication rule: 4 × (4 − 1) × (4 − 2) = 24 ways
