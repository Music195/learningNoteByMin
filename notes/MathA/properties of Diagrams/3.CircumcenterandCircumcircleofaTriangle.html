Geometry â€¢ Triangle Properties
The three perpendicular bisectors of the sides of a triangle intersect at a single point.
This point is called the circumcenter of the triangle.
The circle passing through all three vertices with this point as its center is called the circumcircle of the triangle.
The circumcenter is equidistant from all three vertices of the triangle.
The circumcenter $O$ of $\triangle ABC$ is equidistant from all three vertices $A$, $B$, and $C$.
