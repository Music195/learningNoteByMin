Incenter & Angle Bisectors (内心・内接円)

Triangle Geometry
In any triangle, the three angle bisectors always intersect at a single point.
This intersection point is called the incenter (点 I).

The circle centered at I and tangent to all three sides is called the incircle.

Properties:
  • The incenter is equidistant from all three sides of the triangle.
  • The incircle touches each side exactly once.
The incenter I is the intersection of the three angle bisectors.
The incircle is tangent to all three sides.
The incenter is equidistant from all three sides.
💡 Fact: The incenter I is always inside the triangle, and the incircle is the largest circle that fits inside the triangle.
