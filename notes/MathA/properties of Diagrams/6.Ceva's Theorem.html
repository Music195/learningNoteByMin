Ceva's Theorem (チェバの定理)

Triangle Geometry
In triangle ABC, let point O be inside the triangle.
Draw lines from each vertex (A, B, C) through O to the opposite sides, meeting at points P, Q, R respectively.

Ceva's Theorem states:
\( \displaystyle \frac{BP}{PC} \cdot \frac{CQ}{QA} \cdot \frac{AR}{RB} = 1 \)
Lines from each vertex through O meet the opposite sides at P, Q, R.
💡
  • The points P, Q, R divide the sides in certain ratios.
  • If the product of these ratios equals 1, the lines are concurrent.
  • Ceva's Theorem works for any point O inside the triangle.
  • It is a fundamental result in triangle geometry and useful for proving concurrency.
