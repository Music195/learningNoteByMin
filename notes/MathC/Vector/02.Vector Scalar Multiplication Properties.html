Properties of Scalar Multiplication of Vectors
Let \( k,\, l \) be real numbers.
1.   \( k(l\vec{a}) = \) \( (kl)\vec{a} \)
2.   \( (k + l)\vec{a} = \) \( k\vec{a} + l\vec{a} \)
3.   \( k(\vec{a} + \vec{b}) = \) \( k\vec{a} + k\vec{b} \)
By the above properties and the properties of vector addition,
calculations involving addition, subtraction, and scalar multiplication of vectors can be handled just like algebraic expressions.
