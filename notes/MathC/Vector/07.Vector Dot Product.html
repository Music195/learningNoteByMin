Vector Dot Product
Assuming \( \vec{a} \) and \( \vec{b} \) are not zero vectors, and \( \theta \) is the angle between them:

$$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}| |\vec{b}| \cos\theta $$
When \( \vec{a} = (a_1, a_2) \) and \( \vec{b} = (b_1, b_2) \):

$$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_1b_1 + a_2b_2 $$
If \( \vec{a} = \vec{0} \) or \( \vec{b} = \vec{0} \), the dot product of \( \vec{a} \) and \( \vec{b} \) is defined as \( \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 0 \).
