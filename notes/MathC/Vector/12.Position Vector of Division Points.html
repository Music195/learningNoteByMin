Position Vector of Internal and External Division Points
For two points \( A(\vec{a}) \) and \( B(\vec{b}) \), the position vector of the point that divides the line segment AB in the ratio \( m:n \) is:

Internal Division

$$ \frac{n\vec{a} + m\vec{b}}{m+n} $$

External Division

$$ \frac{-n\vec{a} + m\vec{b}}{m-n} $$
In particular, the position vector of the midpoint of the line segment AB is: $$ \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b}}{2} $$
Note: The formula for external division can be obtained by replacing \( n \) with \( -n \) in the internal division formula.
