13. Collinearity and Perpendicularity of Three Points

For three distinct points \( A(\alpha) \), \( B(\beta) \), \( C(\gamma) \):

Collinear:
The points \( A, B, C \) are collinear if and only if $$ \frac{\gamma - \alpha}{\beta - \alpha} $$ is a real number.
Perpendicular:
The lines \( AB \) and \( AC \) are perpendicular if and only if $$ \frac{\gamma - \alpha}{\beta - \alpha} $$ is a pure imaginary number.
