4. Properties of Conics by Eccentricity

For a fixed point \( F \) (focus), a fixed line \( \ell \) (not passing through \( F \)), and a constant \( e > 0 \), the locus of points \( P \) such that

\( \dfrac{\text{distance from } P \text{ to } F}{\text{distance from } P \text{ to } \ell} = e \)
  • [1] \( 0 < e < 1 \) : Ellipse
  • [2] \( e = 1 \) : Parabola
  • [3] \( e > 1 \) : Hyperbola
The value \( e \) is called the eccentricity of the conic, and the line \( \ell \) is called the directrix.
