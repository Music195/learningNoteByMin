5. Polar and Cartesian Coordinates

Let the Cartesian coordinates of point \( P \) be \( (x, y) \), and the polar coordinates be \( (r, \theta) \).

  1. \( x = r \cos \theta,\quad y = r \sin \theta \)
  2. \( r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \)
    \( \cos \theta = \dfrac{x}{r},\quad \sin \theta = \dfrac{y}{r} \quad (r \neq 0) \)
When considering polar coordinates in the coordinate plane, the origin \( O \) is usually taken as the pole, and the positive \( x \)-axis as the initial line.
