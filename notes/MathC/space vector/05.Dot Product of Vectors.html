5. Dot Product of Vectors

Let \(\vec{a} = (a_1,\, a_2,\, a_3)\),â€ƒ\(\vec{b} = (b_1,\, b_2,\, b_3)\) be nonzero vectors.
Let the angle between them be \(\theta\).

1. Dot Product Formula

The dot product (inner product) of \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\) is:

$$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_1 b_1 + a_2 b_2 + a_3 b_3 $$

2. Dot Product and Angle

The cosine of the angle \(\theta\) between \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\) is given by:

$$ \cos\theta = \frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{a}|\,|\vec{b}|} $$

Substituting the formulas for the dot product and magnitudes:

$$ \cos\theta = \frac{a_1 b_1 + a_2 b_2 + a_3 b_3} {\sqrt{a_1^2 + a_2^2 + a_3^2}\;\sqrt{b_1^2 + b_2^2 + b_3^2}} $$
Notes:
  • The dot product is a scalar (not a vector).
  • If \(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 0\), then \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\) are perpendicular (orthogonal).
  • The formula for \(\cos\theta\) allows you to find the angle between two vectors.
  • The dot product is widely used in geometry, physics (work, projection), and computer graphics.
