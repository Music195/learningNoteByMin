7. Position Vectors and Section Formula

1. Section Formula (Internal and External Division)

For two points \(A(\vec{a})\), \(B(\vec{b})\), the position vector of the point dividing segment \(AB\) in the ratio \(m:n\) is:

Internal Division: $$ \frac{m\vec{a} + n\vec{b}}{m + n} $$ External Division: $$ \frac{-m\vec{a} + n\vec{b}}{m - n} $$

Midpoint: The position vector of the midpoint of \(AB\) is:

$$ \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b}}{2} $$

2. Centroid of Triangle

For triangle \(ABC\) with vertices \(A(\vec{a})\), \(B(\vec{b})\), \(C(\vec{c})\), the position vector of the centroid \(G\) is:

$$ \vec{g} = \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c}}{3} $$
Note:
  • These formulas hold in 3D space just as they do in the plane.
  • The section formula is useful for finding points that divide a segment in a given ratio.
  • The centroid formula gives the average position of the three vertices of a triangle.
