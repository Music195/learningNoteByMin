8. Distance Between Two Points and Section Points

1. Distance Between Two Points

For points \(A(a_1,\, a_2,\, a_3)\) and \(B(b_1,\, b_2,\, b_3)\), the distance \(AB\) is:

$$ AB = \sqrt{(b_1 - a_1)^2 + (b_2 - a_2)^2 + (b_3 - a_3)^2} $$

2. Section Points (Internal and External Division)

The coordinates of the point dividing segment \(AB\) in the ratio \(m:n\) are:

Internal Division: $$ \left( \frac{n a_1 + m b_1}{m + n},\; \frac{n a_2 + m b_2}{m + n},\; \frac{n a_3 + m b_3}{m + n} \right) $$ External Division: $$ \left( \frac{-n a_1 + m b_1}{m - n},\; \frac{-n a_2 + m b_2}{m - n},\; \frac{-n a_3 + m b_3}{m - n} \right) $$
Notes:
  • The distance formula gives the straight-line distance between two points in 3D space.
  • The section formulas (internal and external) are useful for finding points that divide a segment in a given ratio.
  • These formulas are valid in both 2D and 3D.
